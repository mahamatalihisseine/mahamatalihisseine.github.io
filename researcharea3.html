Research Area: Data Anonymization

Overview

Research in Data Anonymization focuses on developing robust methods for preserving privacy in digital environments. By applying advanced techniques like data masking, generalization, and k‑anonymity, this area ensures sensitive information can be shared, analyzed, and leveraged securely while protecting the privacy of individuals and organizations.

Research Focus Areas

  • Advanced Techniques for Personal Identifiable Information (PII) Anonymization
  • Differential Privacy and K‑Anonymity Frameworks
  • Data Encryption and De‑identification for Big Data and AI
  • Anonymization Strategies for Blockchain and Distributed Systems

Technologies & Frameworks

Research focuses on leveraging advanced privacy-preserving techniques such as Differential Privacy, Secure Multiparty Computation (MPC), k‑Anonymity, and Homomorphic Encryption. These methods enable data utilization in AI, big data analytics, and decentralized environments while ensuring privacy and compliance with global data regulations like GDPR and CCPA.

Current works

  • Anonymization Techniques for Secured Medical Records in Distributed Systems
  • Differentially Private Algorithms for Big Data Analysis
  • Decentralized Identity Management and Anonymization Techniques for Smart Cities
