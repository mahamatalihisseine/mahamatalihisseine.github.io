Blockchain‑Based Handle System for Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC)

Overview

This project proposes a new approach to managing identifiers within Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC) ecosystems using a Blockchain‑Based Handle System. The goal is to enable trusted, decentralized identifier resolution that can support digital currency issuance, verification, and secure access across stakeholders in national and international financial environments.

Project Objectives

  • Establish a secure and persistent identifier framework for CBDC transactions
  • Enhance privacy and data protection in digital currency ecosystems
  • Enable interoperability across banking institutions and jurisdictions
  • Integrate Handle System with distributed ledger technologies for seamless access
  • Support trust and compliance across national and international financial stakeholders

Technologies & Frameworks

This project uses a combination of state‑of‑the‑art technologies to enable decentralized identifier management for CBDCs:

  • Blockchain platforms (Hyperledger, Ethereum)
  • Handle System for persistent digital identifiers
  • Decentralized Identity Frameworks (DIDs)
  • Smart Contracts for transaction verification
  • IPFS for off‑chain data storage and retrieval

Summary

With a Blockchain‑Based Handle System, CBDCs can achieve a new level of trust, privacy, and accessibility. By leveraging distributed identifiers, this approach allows central banks to operate a resilient, user‑centric digital currency that is secure, privacy‑aware, and adaptable for future global commerce and banking requirements.
