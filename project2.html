Project 2: Unified Blockchain Handle System (UBHS)

Overview

The UBHS project proposes a next‑generation Blockchain‑based Handle System Architecture designed for seamless cross‑domain interoperability. Its goal is to create a robust, decentralized identifier and access framework, addressing complex disaster management scenarios (such as floods) across four application domains: Education, Industrial IoT, Data Sharing, and Social Media Research.

Research Focus Areas

Education

Ensure long‑term trust and traceability for academic records and digital credentials across institutions and borders.

Industrial IoT

Provide secure, decentralized access and identifier resolution for connected IoT devices in manufacturing environments.

Data Sharing

Foster trusted and privacy‑aware data access across organizations, researchers, and industries using UBHS identifiers.

Social Media Research

Provide persistent identifiers for digital content and user profiles, supporting research and long‑term access in social media platforms.

Summary

The UBHS advances the Handle System by providing a highly interoperable, blockchain‑based identifier architecture. Through four subsystems — Education, Industrial IoT, Data Sharing, and Social Media Research — UBHS aims to create a robust, secure, and trusted framework for managing digital resources across diverse domains, making it ideal for disaster recovery and resilient data access in crisis scenarios like floods.
